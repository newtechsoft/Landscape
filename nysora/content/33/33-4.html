Equipment needed includes the following:

  • Ultrasound machine with linear transducer (8–14 MHz), sterile sleeve, and gel
  • Standard nerve block tray
  • One 20-mL syringe containing local anesthetic
  • A 2-in, 22–25 gauge short-bevel insulated stimulating needle
  • Peripheral nerve stimulator (optional)
  • Sterile gloves

Tip:

Because these are superficial blocks of distal peripheral nerves, some practitioners choose to use a small-gauge (i.e., 25-gauge) needle.

When a using small-gauge needle, however, meticulous attention should be paid to avoid an intraneural injection, which is more likely with a smaller diameter and sharp tip design.
